Consider the points on an infinite grid of equilateral triangles as shown below:

Note that if we number the points from left to right and from top to bottom, then groups of these points form the vertices of certain geometric shapes. For example, the list of points [1,2,3] and [7,9,18] are the vertices of triangles, the sets [11,13,26,24] and [2, 3, 5, 4] are the vertices of parallelograms (lozenge), and the sets [4,5,9,13,12,7] and [4,6,15,26,24,11] are the vertices of regular hexagons.

Write a program which will continuously accept a set of points on this triangular grid, analyze it, and determine whether the points are the vertices of an "acceptable" figure: triangle, parallelogram (lozenge), or a hexagon. In order for a figure to be acceptable, it must meet the following three conditions:

  1. 1) Each side of the figure must coincide with an edge on the grid.
  2. 2) All sides of the figure must be the same length.
  3. 3) The figure must be convex (has a regular shape).

Your program should deduce which geometric figure is represented from the number of points within your input set (e.g., six points can only represent a hexagon, etc).

Input: A list of no more than six points. The points are limited to a range of 1 through 32767.

Output: An integer: 3 for a triangle, 4 for a parallelogram, 6 for a regular hexagon and 0 if the figure is not acceptable. The listed points may be given in any order.
